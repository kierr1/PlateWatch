Upload Microscope Image for Platelet Count
Drop microscope image here JPG, PNG, TIFF · max 20 MB · 40× or 100× field recommended
How it works: The AI detects individual platelet cells in your microscope image — the small, oval, purple-staining fragments visible under 40× magnification. Each platelet is circled and numbered. The field count is multiplied by a calibration factor to estimate the total platelet density per µL of blood, then compared against WHO dengue thresholds.

About PlateletWatch

PlateletWatch is an AI-assisted platelet counting system designed for dengue fever monitoring in resource-limited clinical settings. It uses computer vision to detect and count platelets in blood smear microscope images.

AI Detection

Computer vision model trained on thousands of annotated blood smear images to detect platelets at 40× and 100× magnification.

WHO Thresholds

Results are automatically classified against WHO dengue severity guidelines: normal (>150k), low (50–150k), danger (20–50k), critical (<20k).

Clinical Validation

Validated against manual CBC counts at Philippine General Hospital. For clinical decision support only — not a replacement for laboratory CBC.
